PEAS Framework

ComponentDescription
Performance Measures Maximize diagnostic accuracy, minimize false negatives and false positives, detect critical conditions promptly, provide explainable predictions, achieve high confidence when sufficient data is available, generate timely recommendations.
Environment Patient demographics (age, gender), medical history, pre-existing conditions, blood pressure status, cholesterol levels, symptoms, lifestyle factors, vital signs, and other health-related information provided by the patient.
Actuators Generate ranked disease predictions with confidence scores, display risk/danger levels, provide diagnostic explanations, recommend medical tests, issue emergency alerts for critical conditions, suggest when to seek medical attention.
Sensors Body Temperature Input, Heart Rate Input, Blood Pressure Input (systolic/diastolic in mmHg), Oxygen Saturation (SpO₂), Symptom Checklist, Blood Sugar Level, Medical History & Demographics, Uploaded Health Reports (lab results, ECG scans, doctor notes), Camera (skin colour, eye assessment).
ComponentDescription
Performance Accurate autonomous measurement of vitals within clinical tolerance · Real-time anomaly detection (arrhythmia, SpO₂ drop, fever spike) · Minimise patient discomfort and sensor contact time · Transmit structured data to inference engine within 2 seconds of reading
Environment Physical patient body (skin, veins, airways, eyes); clinical or home environment (lighting, ambient noise, temperature); patient movement and cooperation level; prior readings stored in onboard or cloud memory.
Actuators Inflatable BP cuff, pulse oximeter clip, infrared thermometer, ECG electrode array, motorised arm for patient positioning, audio/visual display panel for patient feedback
Sensors Infrared Temperature Sensor, Heart Rate & Rhythm Sensor (ECG leads + PPG), Blood Pressure Transducer, SpO₂ / Pulse Oximetry Sensor, Blood Glucose Sensor, Respiratory Sound Microphone, Camera / Visual Sensor.
